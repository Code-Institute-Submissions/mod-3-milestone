History of the band

In 1965 Bob Rafelson and Bert Schneider introduced the world to Davy Jones born 30th December 1945 in Manchester UK, Micky Dolenz born 8th March 1945 in Los Angeles USA, Peter Tork born 13th February 1942 in Washington USA and Michael Nesmith born 30th December 1942 in Texas USA.

These four aspiring musical actors who are now known and loved by all as “The Monkees”.

"The Monkees" which aired from 1966-1968 beginning their global success until the band decided to split in 1971. This however was not to be the end, in 1986 a revival of interest in the television show led to reunion tours and new record releases from the band, contributing to the sale of over 75 million record sales worldwide.

It is reported that in 1967 The Monkees outsold the Beatles and the Rolling Stones combined with international hits including “Last Train to Clarksville”, “Pleasant Valley Sunday”, “Daydream Believer” and “I’m a Believer”.
